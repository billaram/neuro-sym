# Bibliography

## Week 1 — Foundations

1. **Launchbury, John — "A DARPA Perspective on Artificial Intelligence" (2017)**
   - Type: Presentation / Video
   - Author: John Launchbury, Director of DARPA I2O
   - Key topics: Wave-1 (handcrafted), Wave-2 (statistical), Wave-3 (contextual adaptation)
   - Summary & slides: https://machinelearning.technicacuriosa.com/2017/03/19/a-darpa-perspective-on-artificial-intelligence/
   - Detailed breakdown: https://brianhhu.github.io/2017-05-03-three-waves-ai/
   - PDF: `research/papers/week1/`

1a. **Sheth, Amit — "The Duality of Data and Knowledge Across the Three Waves of AI" (2021)**
   - Type: Companion paper formalizing the DARPA three-waves framework
   - arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.13520
   - PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2103.13520
   - PDF: `research/papers/week1/`

2. **Besold, Garcez et al. — "Neural-Symbolic Learning and Reasoning: A Survey and Interpretation" (2017)**
   - Type: Survey paper (58 pages, 14 authors)
   - Key topics: Integration of neural and symbolic AI, taxonomy of approaches, applications in computational biology, fault diagnosis, software verification
   - arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.03902
   - PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.03902
   - Semantic Scholar: https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/9dbb506ded56ff4b7ab65aa92b363c0112987f10
   - PDF: `research/papers/week1/`

3. **Reed, Scott & de Freitas, Nando — "Neural Programmer-Interpreters" (ICLR 2016, Best Paper)**
   - Type: Conference paper
   - Key topics: Compositional program learning, recurrent core + program memory + domain encoders, learning to execute programs from traces
   - arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1511.06279
   - PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1511.06279
   - Author project page: http://www.scottreed.info/iclr_project.html
   - Lecture slides (U of T): https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~florian/courses/imitation_learning/lectures/Lecture5.pdf
   - PDF: `research/papers/week1/`

4. **Wei, Jason et al. — "Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models" (NeurIPS 2022)**
   - Type: Conference paper
   - Authors: Jason Wei, Xuezhi Wang, Dale Schuurmans, Maarten Bosma, Ed Chi, Quoc Le, Denny Zhou (Google Brain)
   - Key topics: Step-by-step reasoning via prompting, arithmetic/commonsense/symbolic reasoning, 540B model achieves SOTA on GSM8K with 8 exemplars
   - arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903
   - PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2201.11903
   - NeurIPS proceedings: https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2022/hash/9d5609613524ecf4f15af0f7b31abca4-Abstract-Conference.html
   - Google Research blog: https://research.google/blog/language-models-perform-reasoning-via-chain-of-thought/
   - PDF: `research/papers/week1/`
